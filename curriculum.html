Montessori Curriculum

Discover our comprehensive five-area curriculum designed to nurture the whole child through hands-on learning and exploration.

Comprehensive Learning Through Five Key Areas

Our authentic Montessori curriculum is carefully designed around five core areas that work together to provide a complete educational experience. Each area builds upon the others, creating a foundation for lifelong learning and development.

Practical Life

The foundation of Montessori education, where children develop independence, concentration, and real-world skills through meaningful daily activities.

The Practical Life curriculum incorporates exercises and activities that children observe in daily life. These activities develop children's independence, concentration, and fine motor skills while building confidence and self-esteem.

Spooning & Scooping

Developing hand-eye coordination and precision

Food Preparation

Learning kitchen skills and healthy eating habits

Grace & Courtesy

Social skills and respectful communication

Cleaning Tasks

Care of environment and responsibility

Plant Care

Nurturing living things and understanding growth

Threading

Fine motor development and concentration

Mathematics

Making abstract mathematical concepts concrete through specially designed materials that children can see, touch, and manipulate.

The Mathematics curriculum teaches children to understand abstract mathematical concepts through hands-on learning experiences. Children learn to count, identify numerals, and understand mathematical operations using beautiful, concrete materials.

Numerals & Counters

Connecting quantity to symbol

Hanging Bead Stair

Visual representation of quantities 1-10

Teen Boards

Understanding numbers 11-19

Hundred Board

Number patterns and sequences to 100

Addition Strip Board

Concrete introduction to addition

Golden Beads

Decimal system and place value

Language

Building strong communication skills through phonetic learning, vocabulary development, and the joy of reading and writing.

The Language curriculum provides children with knowledge and skills to build vocabulary and understanding. Children develop reading, writing, and oral language skills through hands-on experience with carefully designed materials.

Sandpaper Letters

Tactile learning of letter sounds and formation

Moveable Alphabet

Building words before mastering writing

Metal Insets

Preparing for writing through tracing

Three Part Cards

Vocabulary building and reading practice

Phonics Games

Sound recognition and blending

Reading Books

Progressive reading levels and comprehension

Sensorial

Refining the senses and developing the ability to classify, organize, and discriminate through concrete experiences.

Sensorial activities teach children to refine their senses and organize sensory impressions. These materials prepare children for mathematics, language, and geometry by teaching classification, ordering, and discrimination.

Pink Tower

Visual discrimination of dimension

Color Boxes

Color recognition and gradation

Geometric Solids

Three-dimensional shape exploration

Trinomial Cube

Algebraic concepts through manipulation

Red Rods

Length discrimination and comparison

Sound Cylinders

Auditory discrimination and matching

Culture

Exploring the interconnectedness of all things through geography, science, history, art, and music, fostering global awareness and environmental stewardship.

The Culture curriculum incorporates geography, botany, zoology, science, history, music, and art. Children develop understanding of their community, world, and social responsibilities while learning to appreciate diversity.

Land & Water Forms

Geographic vocabulary and concepts

Continent Maps

World geography and cultural awareness

Botany Cabinet

Plant classification and life cycles

Animal Classification

Zoology and animal characteristics

Science Experiments

Hands-on exploration of natural phenomena

Music & Art

Creative expression and cultural appreciation

Continuous Assessment & Progress Tracking

Our approach to assessment honors the individual development of each child. We use observation-based assessment methods that provide meaningful insights into your child's learning journey.

Rather than traditional testing, our teachers carefully observe and document each child's progress, interests, and developmental milestones throughout their time with us.

  • Daily observation and documentation
  • Individual learning portfolios
  • Regular parent conferences
  • Developmental milestone tracking
  • Photographic learning records
  • Transition readiness assessments
